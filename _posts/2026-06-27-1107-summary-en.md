## [Classic physics question on kinetic energy's quadratic relationship resurfaces on Hacker News](https://physics.stackexchange.com/questions/535/why-does-kinetic-energy-increase-quadratically-not-linearly-with-speed) ⭐️ 7.0/10

A 2011 Physics Stack Exchange question about why kinetic energy scales with the square of speed resurfaced on Hacker News, sparking a lively discussion with intuitive and mathematical explanations. The discussion highlights a foundational physics concept that is often taken for granted, offering multiple perspectives that can enhance science education and clarify common misconceptions. Key insights included a braking car analogy demonstrating the quadratic effect, a reference to Michael Spivak's rigorous treatment, and a debate on whether energy is conserved in Galilean relativity.

hackernews · ProxyTracer · Jun 26, 22:43 · [Discussion](https://news.ycombinator.com/item?id=48692946)

**Background**: Kinetic energy is the energy an object possesses due to its motion, classically expressed as KE = ½mv². The quadratic relationship arises from the work-energy theorem: the work done by a net force equals the change in kinetic energy, and for constant acceleration from rest, the distance traveled is proportional to the square of the final speed. This contrasts with momentum, which is linear in velocity.

## [Model Router for Coding Agents Saves 40% on Tokens](https://github.com/workweave/router) ⭐️ 6.0/10

Weave Router is a new tool that integrates with coding agents like Claude Code, Codex, and Cursor. It uses reinforcement learning trained on agent traces to dynamically route simpler tasks to cheaper models (e.g., DeepSeek V4, GLM 5.2) and complex tasks to frontier models (e.g., Opus 4.8, GPT 5.5), achieving a 40% reduction in token costs. As AI coding agent adoption grows, model costs can become prohibitive. Intelligent routing like this can significantly cut expenses without sacrificing quality, making AI-assisted development more accessible and sustainable. The router is trained on tens of thousands of agent traces but community feedback warns it breaks prompt caching, which is critical for long coding sessions, and may clash with agents' internal model-aware routing and model-specific prompting strategies.

hackernews · adchurch · Jun 26, 16:40 · [Discussion](https://news.ycombinator.com/item?id=48688700)

**Background**: A model router is middleware that classifies requests by complexity and sends them to appropriate model tiers. Prompt caching stores recent prompt prefixes to speed up responses and reduce costs, but switching models between calls can cause cache misses. Modern coding agents often have built-in routing logic that already delegates subtasks to cheaper or more powerful models based on the task nature.
