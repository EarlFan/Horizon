A third-party 10G Ethernet module for Framework laptops, made by Wisdpi, requires USB 3.2 Gen 2x2 (20Gbps) for full performance, but its implementation exposes the messy state of USB-C standards and compatibility. The post illuminates the pitfalls of USB-C's overly complex standard, which often leads to confusion and compatibility issues for both developers and users, and shows the practical limits of modular laptop expansion. The module uses USB 3.2 Gen 2x2, a rarely supported standard, likely due to cost; sustained 10Gbps performance generates significant heat, challenging laptop thermal budgets; and only systems with the specific USB mode achieve full speed, otherwise it falls back to slower USB 3.2 Gen 2 (10Gbps) or USB 3.0.

**Background**: Framework laptops feature swappable expansion cards that connect via USB-C internally. USB 3.2 Gen 2x2 is a dual-lane 20Gbps USB protocol that uses USB-C connectors, but is not commonly supported by most host devices—unlike USB4 or Thunderbolt. 10 Gigabit Ethernet, standardized by IEEE 802.3ae, delivers 10Gbps throughput and typically requires dedicated PCIe cards with heatsinks due to high power and heat dissipation.

## [Un-0: Coupled Oscillator Image Generation on Conventional Hardware](https://unconv.ai/blog/introducing-un-0-generating-images-with-coupled-oscillators/) ⭐️ 7.0/10

Unconventional AI has introduced Un-0, an open-source image generator that uses a simulated system of coupled oscillators running on conventional hardware. On ImageNet 64×64, it achieves an FID score of 6.74, matching the quality of leading conventional image generation methods at their inception. Un-0 represents a step toward analog computing for AI, potentially offering drastic energy efficiency improvements over digital neural networks. This could help address the growing energy demands of large-scale AI models and stimulate further research into unconventional hardware. Currently, Un-0 is simulated on conventional digital hardware, so its energy efficiency benefits are theoretical until physical oscillator chips are developed. The architecture requires O(n²) connections, which limits scalability—generating a 4K image would need trillions of connections.

hackernews · babelfish · Jun 25, 20:50 · [Discussion](https://news.ycombinator.com/item?id=48679007)

**Background**: Analog computing, which uses continuous physical quantities like voltage or phase to represent information, was once a mainstream approach before digital computers took over. Coupled oscillators are systems of interacting rhythmic elements that naturally synchronize; they can be harnessed to perform computation by mapping problems to their dynamics. This work is part of a broader effort to explore beyond-Moore computing substrates that could offer energy and speed advantages for AI workloads.
