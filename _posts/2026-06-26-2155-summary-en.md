## [First Entire Herculaneum Scroll Read Using AI](https://scrollprize.org/firstscroll) ⭐️ 9.0/10

Researchers have successfully read an entire carbonized scroll from Herculaneum for the first time, using machine learning for segmentation and ink detection. This achievement marks a major milestone in the Vesuvius Challenge. This opens the door to reading hundreds of other unreadable scrolls, potentially recovering lost classical texts. It demonstrates the power of AI in archaeology and cultural heritage preservation. The achievement used deep learning models to detect carbon ink on CT scans, followed by virtual unwrapping and segmentation. The scroll, known as PHerc. Paris. 4, is now fully transcribed.

hackernews · verditelabs · Jun 25, 15:48 · [Discussion](https://news.ycombinator.com/item?id=48675179)

**Background**: The Herculaneum papyri are over 1,800 carbonized scrolls buried by the eruption of Mount Vesuvius in 79 AD. They are the only surviving library from antiquity. Since their discovery in 1752, reading them has been extremely challenging because the scrolls are fragile and the carbon-based ink is hard to distinguish from the charred papyrus. The Vesuvius Challenge was launched to crowdsource AI solutions for virtual unwrapping and ink detection.
